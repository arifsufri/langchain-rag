Peaky Blinders is a British period crime drama television series created by Steven Knight. The show premiered on BBC Two in 2013 and ran for six seasons until 2022.

The series is set in Birmingham, England, and follows the exploits of the Shelby crime family in the aftermath of World War I. The story begins in 1919 and spans several years, depicting the rise of the Shelby family from local street gang to a legitimate business empire.

Main Characters:
- Thomas "Tommy" Shelby: The ambitious leader of the Peaky Blinders, a war veteran with grand ambitions. Played by Cillian Murphy.
- Arthur Shelby: Tommy's older brother, a violent man struggling with PTSD from the war. Played by Paul Anderson.
- Polly Gray: The matriarch of the Shelby family and Tommy's aunt. Played by Helen McCrory.
- Ada Shelby: Tommy's sister who often disapproves of the family's criminal activities. Played by Sophie Rundle.
- John Shelby: Another Shelby brother, fiercely loyal to Tommy. Played by Joe Cole.
- Finn Shelby: The youngest Shelby brother.
- Michael Gray: Polly's son who joins the family business.

The name "Peaky Blinders" refers to the gang's practice of sewing razor blades into the peaks of their flat caps, which they would use as weapons. The show depicts their trademark style including flat caps, long coats, and three-piece suits.

Historical Context:
The show is set during a period of significant social and political change in Britain. The series depicts real historical events and figures, including:
- Winston Churchill
- Oswald Mosley and the rise of fascism
- The economic turmoil of the 1920s and 1930s
- Post-World War I trauma and its impact on society

Themes:
- Family loyalty and betrayal
- The cost of ambition and power
- PTSD and the effects of war
- Class struggle in early 20th century Britain
- The transformation from criminality to legitimacy

The show was praised for its cinematography, acting performances (particularly Cillian Murphy's portrayal of Tommy Shelby), period-accurate sets and costumes, and its modern soundtrack featuring contemporary artists like Nick Cave, Arctic Monkeys, and PJ Harvey.

Notable Storylines:
- The Shelby family's conflicts with rival gangs like the Italians and Billy Kimber's gang
- Tommy's political ambitions and entry into Parliament
- The family's expansion into legitimate businesses including horse racing and automotive industries
- The impact of the Great Depression on their operations
- Battles with the IRA and various government forces

The show concluded with its sixth season in 2022, and a film continuation has been announced to wrap up the story of Tommy Shelby and the Peaky Blinders.
